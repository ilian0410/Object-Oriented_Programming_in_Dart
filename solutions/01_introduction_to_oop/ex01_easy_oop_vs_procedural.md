## Solution

### Answers

1. **Snippet A**: Procedural — data (`name`, `age`) and functions (`printInfo`, `birthday`) are separate.
   **Snippet B**: Object-oriented — data and behavior are grouped inside a `Person` class.
   **Snippet C**: Procedural — data (`radius`, `pi`) and function (`area`) are separate.

2. The key difference: in OOP, data and the methods that operate on that data are **encapsulated together** inside a class. In procedural code, data and functions are independent.

3. Two benefits of OOP:
   - **Reusability**: a `Person` class can be instantiated multiple times, each with its own state.
   - **Maintainability**: changes to how a Person works are localized to the class, not scattered across functions.
